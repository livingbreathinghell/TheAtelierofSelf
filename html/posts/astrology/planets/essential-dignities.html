⋆°˖➴ Planetary Dignities & Debilities ˎˊ˗

A planets dignity (or lack there of) describes the "condition" of the planet/how its influence manifests in the chart.

Dignity and debility describes ones place and recognition in society. A planet with dignity has an accepted place, power, and responsibilities to uphold. A planet without dignity is akin to an outsider - it has a lack of autonomy and is at odds with society.

Essential dignity measures a planet's inherent strength, dictating its capacity to produce intended results. Accidental dignity refers to a planet's situational strength, determining how easily it can express that power.

Essential Dignities and Debilities▼

!! IMPORTANT !!

An important outlier to mention: when a planet has no dignity at all, (not in its domicile, exaltation, triplicity, bounds, or decans) it is called 'peregrine.' it embodies a wanderous energy - akin to pilgrims, foreigners, and travelers. the planets autonomy is diminished, forced to abide by the planet that rules (the zodiac theyre in.) These planets are dubbed "street smart," described as "A homeless outsider used to living by wits." When malefics are peregrin, their evil is increased. when benefics are peregrin, their good is nullified. most people have peregrin planets and there are some megiating factors: accidental dignities, mutual reception, etc..

The sign a planet rules is called its "domicile," or its home. The planet gains dignity, its most comfortable within this sign. it has strength - playing by its own rules with total autonomy and control over its self expression. The results are more auspiscious as the planet can naturally embody its core significations without restraint or pressure.

And from there the rest of the visible planets were assigned to signs in descending order of distance from the Sun: Mercury (Gemini/Virgo), Venus (Taurus/Libra), Mars (Aries/Scorpio), Jupiter (Sagittarius/Pisces), and Saturn (Capricorn/Aquarius). The modern planets were assaigned the following: Uranus to Aquarius, Neptune to Pisces, Pluto to Scorpio.

The meaning of domicile changing with the discovery of modern planets.

Traditionally, domicile is about responsibility and where the planet has easy control - But modernly, it seems to be about affinity. But in defense of modern logic - I can only assume, with Saturn being the veil between the light and dark, what is seen and unseen, its understandable for it to remain in sole ownership over capricorn (the zodiac marking the START of the winter solstice, the veil between the 'light and dark.') The modern planets arnt to be used in a personal context as it can take generations to move from sign to sign - thus it should only be used to describe or analyze generational matters.

The ruling planet has responsibility over the zodiac and how it functions in the chart (the house the zodiac rules, the other planets inside it.) Its equated to the host of the house, while everything inside of it is its guest. Its best the host planet makes an aspects to the guest planet - this means the host planet is supporting the guest planet in some way. If theres no aspect, it means the guest has been totally estranged from the host. When two planets are located in signs ruled by each other, this is called 'mutual reception' - it strengthens both planets by giving them access to each other's resources.

Exaltation is a form of rulership, deemed as the second most important type (behind the domicile ruler, of course.) domicile is considered to have more dignity than exaltation because in domicile, a planet plays by its own rules, in its own structure. The sign and planet are thought of as being made from the same substance, giving it an inherent durability. Being exalted is akin to being at the height of your glory or success - but that sentiment may exceed ones owns capabilities. Exaltation can lack the same foundational substance domicile has, making it less durable and more prone to self-inflataion or delusion.

The exaltation scheme derives from the concept of the zodiac naturally being complementary to the planet exalted there. most academic scholars of the history of astrology thought the exaltations came from the earlier Mesopotamian tradition based on a set of cuneiform tablets, where it seemed to depict the exaltations of the planets that were identified in the later Greek tradition. However, it appears as if it came up very late in the Mesopotamian tradition. With exaltations pre-dating this, it leaves the possibility that the exaltation scheme developed first in the Hellenistic tradition, and then was transmitted into late Mesopotamian Astrology. However, Its also noted that when you overlay the exaltations on the Thema Mundi, all of the planets fell in one of the positive houses (aka: houses configured to be visible to the ascendant via aspects.) Which could indicate this going as far back as domiciles - etched into the original dignity scheme from the start.

Porphyry points out is when you look at the planets in their signs of exaltation, all of the diurnal(/masculine) planets, which are the Sun, Jupiter and Saturn, when in exaltation, are configured to one of their domiciles by trine whereas all of the nocturnal(/feminine) planets when in exaltation, are configured to one of their domiciles by sextile.

5. Triplicity|ೃ༄▼

⋆. The council of the zodiac signs ⋆˚࿔

Triplicity rulership is a grouping of the signs into groups of 4 - the elements. Fire triplicity, Earth Triplicity, Air Triplicity, and Water triplicity. Each triplicity is assigned 3 planetary rulers that ruler over it simutaneously based off sect - akin to the counsel to the zodiac. All 3 have rulership over the triplicity, but the order of such changes based on the sect of the chart. Theres the diurnal lord, nocturnal lord, and participating lord. The participating lord is always last - while the diurnal/nocturnal lord takes primary rulership based off the chart being diurnal or nocturnal. (diurnal lord being first with diurnal charts, nocturnal being first with nocturnal charts.)

The feminine elements are given the feminine planets (aka night sect): Earth/Water Triplicity - Venus, Moon, Mars

The masculine elements are given the masculine planets (aka day sect): Fire/Air Triplicity - Sun, Jupiter, Saturn

Accidental Dignities▼

Accidental Dignities shows us where and how a planet has OCCASION to act within its confines. It can mitigate low debility planets - making its influence less malefic, it can lower the dignity of high-dignified planets - making its influence less helpful, or it can simply be supporting the planet it whatever it may be doing (negatively or positively.)

1. Houses|ೃ༄▼

There are 4 angles in a chart: Ascendant, Descendant, Imum Coeli, Midheaven. the angles are often referred to as the ‘cardinal points’, and they relate directly to the cardinal directions east, south, west and north. Ancient astrologers viewed the cardinal points as the eternal supports to the earth that holds the earth in position. They align with the 1st, 4th, 7th, and 10th house, and was given the name "angular" houses.These were seen as having the utmost importance, the rest of the houses digities being assaigned in descending order from them. The further the distance between a planet and the angle, the less potency it possesses and the more alienation it experiences in its environment.

1. Angular houses

Because these houses are positioned on the angles, theyre seen as acting more powerfully. Whatever it represents will show itself naturally and prominently - its the foundation - the roots - the angles are what hold the (native) up, so the corrosponding houses will define how. the people or matters that they signify are strong and attract a lot of attention.

Succedent houses

These houses follow the Angular houses, making them the second most powerful. These represent how the basis of your life is upheld (opposed to defining what it is specifically.) its the resources that one maintains.

Cadent houses

Cadent houses are last, literally representing how something falls *away* from prominence. these houses represent how the native adapts, where knowledge is integrated into something cohesive before the next cycle.

Some Angular and Cadent houses, despite their given dignity, arnt as "good" or "bad" as it may imply. This is because of 2 reasons: the relationship the house has to its angular house and the angles it makes to the ascendant. Houses that dont aspect the ascendant cant "see" the ascendant (and vice versa.) Since the ascendant represents the native - what isnt seen can only be accessed through indirect means.

3. Motion|ೃ༄▼

Direct, Retrograde, Stationary

The concept of Planetary Motion derives from The luminaries (light bringers), which are always moving direct. This is called the “direction of life”. The planets, however, have the potential to move backwards - this is called being 'retrograde.' retrograde planets are traditionally considered weakened because they move contrary to the “direction of life,” often internalizing or reversing their significations.

A planet becomes stationary when it pauses to change direction between these two states, meaning theres 2 versions: Stationary Direct (when a planet pauses to resume forwards direction), and Stationary Retrograde (when a planet pauses to go retrograde). This is considered to be a potent place of energy in the chart, representing immense focus to the planets' significations in the natives life.

Stationary Direct: The point where a retrograde planet stops and resumes direct motion. This indicates something in the natives life increasing and picking up speed as time goes on.

Stationary Retrograde: The point where a direct planet stops and begins its retrograde phase. This indicates something in the natives life being reversed and decreasing as time goes on.

4. Aspects|ೃ༄▼

Aspects are the planets connections to one another within the chart. Planets can either support eachother or hinder eachother (at varying degrees.) Essential debilities weaken the result while essential dignities strengthen it.

Difficult aspects (opposition or square) represent problems and conflict. The planet itself could have a lot of dignity, but the other would still be in its way. Or, it could be preventing a low-dignified planet from acting at its full capacity, helping to stabilize its otherwise malefic influences.

Positive aspects (Trines and sextile) represent support. Planets could uplift a low-dignified planet, helping to balance it out and help it act constructively. Or, it could enable an (uncontrollable) planets influence - making the situation worse.

theres a slight exception however. "Out of sign aspects" occur when 2 planets are within a close enough degree-based orb to form an aspect, but fall into signs that wouldnt usually make that aspect (i.e. a planet at the very end of a sign, and the planet at the very beginning of another.) It creates tension between signs that are friends, or alliances between signs that are enemies - resulting in a unique energy that can be akin to diplomatic marriages between enemy kingdoms - they must work together besides their fundemental differences.

Conjunctions are the merging of 2 energies. A normal conjunction occures when the planets are within eachothers orb range - But theres another kind of conjunction dubbed a 'co-present' conjunction. This is when 2 (or more) planets occupy the same sign (regardless of degree.) However, a prominent outlier in conjunctions is the sun, whos light is so extreme it has a more prominent effect on the planets close to it.

Our view of anything within 15 degrees of the sun is generally considered "too bright to view." Thus, any planet more than 15 degrees from the sun (but still 'co-present' within the sky) is considered to be 'bright AND visible,' playing an active role in the natives life.

Any placement from 15-8 degrees from the sun is too close to view, giving the conjunction the name "under the suns beams." That planets energy is being hidden, struggling to express itself and be seen.

From 8-0 degrees, the planet is considered "combust." "Combustion" is seen as the planet being burnt out by the suns light, meaning it cannot function - acting senseless, overwhelmed, debilitated.. however, it also indicates a "death and rebirth" process on the horizion.

"Cazimi" is the heart of the Sun, when a planet is making an exact conjunction to the sun. It represents the "rebirth," where the suns rays bless and purify the planet, giving it infinate wisdom to the themes it corrosponds too in the chart. its boosted, empowered, lit by the suns rays - instead of being washed out by them.

the only exceptions to the suns conditioning are the moon, and planets within their domicile/exaltation (as they are said to have have 'special privledges.')
