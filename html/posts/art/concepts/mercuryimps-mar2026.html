Design

i decided to go for more of a literal imp for a few reasons..

The shape of the glyph inspired the general shape for the imp - with the wings positioned on the lower back to match the line going through the bottom of the glyph.

Mercury if often referred too as "the devils advocate", so i felt more comfortable giving it an actual imp design opposed to mars (which i imagine to be more like a stereotypical "hell.")

The actual glyph for mercury is also.. literally venus with devil horns.. i know a lot of beginner astrologers (including myself) got these 2 mixed up in the start of their studies because of their similarities. So, i knew i wanted something cute - specifically something imitating cuteness

The mask was a no-brainer given previous significations, and mercuries reputation for "dual truths" and "switching sides."

Colors

mercury itself looks dull and grey (to the point where photos of it are often confused with the moon!) however, enchanced color imaging gives us a bright and colorful version. mercury itself is the most impressionable planet - its role differs based on the conditions its in. so, i decided to mimick this and make their base desaturated, only giving them color based on their 'conditions' (or job, lol.)

i liked the idea of a dark-gray body with cream accents - it stays true to the logic and they are versatile colors that can match anything; it leaves their design as impressionable as the planet. i reused this for everything but the mask, which i chose to make a blue-ish shade of white to contrast the warmer shades used for their base design. I hoped this would make it looks more "plastic" in comparison - like an add-on.
